## Likely Questions & Suggested Answers

### 1. "Walk us through a campaign you've managed in a competitive B2C market."
Use the **cosmetic dental clinic** example (PodDigital) or **Medizen Aesthetics**. Structure as:
- **Situation:** Cosmetic dental/aesthetics clinic, high CPCs (£5-15+), competitive local market
- **Challenge:** Needed to reduce CPL while maintaining lead quality — low-quality enquiries waste clinic time
- **Action:** Restructured campaigns by treatment type, implemented negative keyword framework, A/B tested ad copy with treatment-specific messaging, adjusted bid strategy with proper target CPA
- **Result:** Reduced CPL while improving lead quality (qualification rate). Emphasise: "I focused on patient enquiries that actually converted to bookings, not just form fills."

### 2. "What's your experience with dental/medical PPC specifically?"
- "I've managed Google Ads for a cosmetic dental clinic — implants, veneers, cosmetic treatments. I can't name the client as they're still with the agency, but I can discuss the approach in detail."
- "I also managed Medizen Aesthetics in Birmingham — a medically-led cosmetic clinic with injectable treatments and laser procedures."
- "The dynamics are identical to your market: high CPCs, long patient decision cycle, price sensitivity, and the critical distinction between lead volume and lead quality."

### 3. "How would you approach our account in the first month?"
Walk through Slides 5 and 6. Key points:
- Week 1: Full audit — I don't make changes before I understand the account
- Quick wins first: negative keywords, conversion tracking, bid strategy guardrails
- Then restructure by treatment type
- "I'd want to understand your current cost per lead, which treatments drive the most value, and where the drop-off points are in the patient journey."

### 4. "How do you handle managing campaigns across multiple clinics?"
Walk through Slide 8. Key points:
- Separate campaigns per clinic with tailored local messaging
- Shared negative keyword lists — a blocked term benefits every clinic
- Budget allocation based on performance — not equal distribution
- Centralised reporting with clinic-level drill-down
- "Each clinic gets individual attention, but benefits from the collective data."

### 5. "What's ACT? How does it help?"
Keep it brief — Slide 9:
- "I built a proprietary optimization engine that automates the repetitive checks — negative keywords, bid adjustments, performance monitoring"
- "It runs 30+ checks overnight across 6 levels of the account"
- "Every action is logged and explainable — nothing happens without a human reviewing it"
- "This means your campaigns are being optimised continuously, not just when I log in"
- Don't oversell — frame it as a tool that makes you more efficient, not a replacement for expertise

### 6. "What budget levels have you managed?"
- "Accounts ranging from £5,000 to £400,000 per month"
- "Over £50 million in total managed spend across 100+ accounts"
- "Notable clients include Lexus, Toyota, Virgin Media O2, Destinology"
- "I'm equally comfortable with your partner clinic budgets (likely £1-5K/month) and the flagship account"

### 7. "How do you measure success?"
- "Patient enquiries that convert to bookings — not clicks, not impressions, not form fills that go nowhere"
- "CPL is the primary metric, but quality-adjusted CPL is what really matters"
- "I also track: conversion rate by campaign/device/time, Quality Score trends, impression share for high-intent terms, and cost per booked appointment when that data is available"

### 8. "What's your experience with Meta/Facebook Ads?"
- Be honest: your strength is Google Ads, but you have Meta experience
- "Google Search is where high-intent dental patients are — someone searching 'dental implants London' is much further down the funnel than someone scrolling Facebook"
- "Meta is valuable for remarketing (people who visited but didn't enquire) and awareness for premium treatments like Vivo Bridge"
- "I'd recommend a Search-led strategy with Meta supporting the patient journey"

### 9. "What would you change about our current setup?" (if they show you anything)
- Don't trash the current setup — you haven't seen the account
- "I'd need to run the full audit before making specific recommendations, but the areas I always look at first are: conversion tracking setup, bid strategy configuration, negative keyword coverage, and campaign structure by treatment type"
- "Based on my experience with similar dental accounts, the biggest quick wins are usually in negative keywords and bid strategy guardrails"

### 10. "What's your availability / notice period?"
- "I'm available to start immediately"
- "I can have the first audit report delivered within one week of getting account access"

## Things to Avoid

1. **Don't trash their current setup.** You haven't seen it. Say: "I'd need to audit before making specific recommendations."
2. **Don't overpromise.** Say "20-30% CPL reduction within 90 days" not "I'll halve your costs."
3. **Don't get too deep on ACT.** It's a differentiator, not the pitch. 60 seconds max. If they ask more, great — but don't lead with it.
4. **Don't oversell Meta.** This role is Search-led. Position Google Ads as your core strength.
5. **Don't use too much jargon.** Tommaso is a Commercial Director, not a PPC specialist. Explain terms when you use them.
6. **Don't mention specific agency names** from your past or theirs. Keep it professional.
7. **Don't discuss salary/rate first.** Let them bring it up. If pressed: "I'm open to discussing a package that reflects the scope and accountability of the role."
8. **Don't ignore Giulio.** He's in marketing — include him. Ask him questions too. Make eye contact with both on Teams.
