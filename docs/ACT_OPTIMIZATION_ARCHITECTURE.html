Budget Allocation & Account Health

The account level sits above everything. Before optimizing any campaign, ACT answers: "Is the money in the right place?"

👤 Client Personas
Every client falls into one of two personas. The persona determines the primary KPI and optimization model.

Lead Gen / Services

Search campaigns PMax campaigns Retargeting (Display/YouTube)

Campaign Roles & Budget Bands

Every campaign is assigned a role. The role determines budget priority and which optimization rules apply.

🏷️ Campaign Roles
Assigned via naming convention prefix. Manually set by the account manager to eliminate any ambiguity.
[BD]
Brand Defence
Protect brand terms. Always on. Success = impression share.
[CP]
Core Performer
Money-makers. Primary KPI applies. Gets bulk of budget.
[RT]
Retargeting
Warm audiences. Should have best CPA/ROAS. Audience-limited.
[PR]
Prospecting
New customer acquisition. Higher CPA/lower ROAS tolerance.
[TS]
Testing
Fixed budget cap. Learning phase. Never auto-increase.
Naming convention examples: [BD] Brand Terms · [CP] Shopping - Shoes · [RT] Display Remarketing · [PR] Search - Generic Terms · [TS] New Landing Page Test
💰 Budget Allocation Bands
Each role has a min/max % of total budget. ACT moves budget freely within bands. Breaching bands requires human approval. Defaults editable per client.

Budget priority order: 1. Brand Defence gets what it needs first → 2. Core Performers get the bulk (ranked by efficiency) → 3. Retargeting gets proportional share → 4. Prospecting gets what's left → 5. Testing gets a fixed cap — never auto-increased.
⚖️ Within-Role Budget Competition
When multiple campaigns share the same role, how does ACT split budget between them? The answer depends on how many campaigns share the role.
1 Campaign in Role
Gets 100% of the role's allocation
No competition. The single campaign receives the entire budget band for that role.
E.g., 1× [BD] Brand campaign → gets the full 2-8% brand budget.
2 Campaigns in Role
Ranked by efficiency. Best performer gets up to 65%.
The better performing campaign (by CPA or ROAS) gets the larger share. But the weaker campaign still gets at least 35% to maintain its ability to compete and recover.
E.g., 2× [CP] Shopping campaigns → best ROAS gets 65%, other gets 35% of the CP band.
3+ Campaigns in Role
Ranked by efficiency. No single campaign gets >50%.
Prevents one campaign from starving the others. Budget is distributed proportionally by efficiency score, with a 50% cap on any single campaign. Ensures underperformers still get enough budget to be evaluated fairly.
E.g., 3× [CP] Shopping campaigns → best gets 50%, second gets 30%, third gets 20%.
Why the cap matters: A campaign having a bad week shouldn't be starved of budget entirely. It might recover. If ACT keeps cutting its budget, it creates a death spiral — less budget means less data, which means worse performance, which means even less budget. The minimum share prevents this.

Signal Decomposition

CPA and ROAS are outcome metrics — the result, not the cause. ACT decomposes them into input signals to understand what changed before acting.

📊 Persona 1: Lead Gen — Two Signals
CPA can only change if CPC changes, CVR changes, or both. ACT checks which one moved before deciding how to respond.

AOV caution rule: If ROAS outperformance is driven solely by AOV increase, ACT does NOT auto-recommend budget increases. AOV spikes can be caused by a single large order — scaling budget based on that would waste money. ACT monitors and waits for the trend to confirm over the 30-day AOV window.
⏱️ Signal Detection Windows
Each signal has its own detection window. More volatile signals get longer windows to avoid false alarms.
📈
CPC
7-day window
Changes fast due to auction dynamics. Needs to be caught early before budget is wasted.
🔄
Conversion Rate
14-day window
Noisy day-to-day. A single day with 0 conversions doesn't mean CVR has dropped — needs smoothing.
🛒
Avg Order Value
30-day window
Most volatile signal. One large order can skew a whole week. Ecommerce persona only.

Performance Scoring

ACT rates each campaign's overall health using a weighted blend of three time windows. This score determines budget allocation ranking within a campaign role.

📊 Multi-Window Weighted Blend
Recent data is weighted most heavily, but longer-term performance provides stability and prevents overreaction.
50%Last 7 days
30%Last 14 days
20%Last 30 days
7 Days — 50% Weight
Catches recent trend changes. If a campaign tanked this week, ACT responds quickly.
14 Days — 30% Weight
Smooths out weekly variation. Prevents overreacting to a single bad week.
30 Days — 20% Weight
Baseline stability. Protects campaigns with one bad week after three strong weeks.
How scoring and signal detection work together: Signal detection (CPC 7d, CVR 14d, AOV 30d) tells ACT what's wrong. Performance scoring (7/14/30 blend) tells ACT how to rank campaigns for budget allocation. Both run in parallel.

Actions & Morning Review

Every recommendation falls into one of three categories. Budget changes execute overnight and are reviewed each morning.

⚡ Three Action Types
Categorised by whether the issue is within Google Ads control.
✅

Act

ACT can execute this. Within Google Ads control.
  • Budget shifts between campaigns
  • Bid adjustments
  • Negative keyword additions
  • Budget increases for scaling
Executed overnight, reviewed in morning.
🔍

Investigate

Partially within control. Needs human analysis first.
  • CVR drops — check search terms first
  • If search terms fine → likely website issue
  • Quality score declines
  • Landing page performance
Flagged for morning review with diagnostic data.
🚨

Alert

Outside Google Ads control. Human must investigate externally.
  • AOV shifts (product/inventory issues)
  • Multiple signals worsening
  • Whole account underperforming
  • Website/Merchant Center problems
No automated action. Notification only.

Guardrails & Safety Limits

Hard limits that ACT cannot breach without human approval. These protect against bad automated decisions.

🛡️ Safety Boundaries
  • Budget bands: ACT cannot move a campaign role below its minimum or above its maximum allocation percentage.
  • Max single move: Maximum 10% of daily budget can be shifted in a single overnight cycle. Larger moves are queued for approval before execution.
  • Within-role cap: No single campaign within a role gets more than 50% of that role's allocation (3+ campaigns) or 65% (2 campaigns). Solo campaigns get 100%.
  • Testing campaigns [TS]: Budget is never auto-increased. Fixed cap set at account setup.
  • Whole account underperformance: If ALL campaigns are outside target range, ACT flags an Account Health Alert and does NOT redistribute — human must investigate externally.
  • AOV-driven outperformance: For ecommerce accounts, if ROAS outperformance is driven solely by AOV increase, ACT monitors but does NOT auto-scale. AOV spikes may be temporary.
